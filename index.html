Aitia

Transparency and accountability in UK personal insolvency and financial vulnerability.

Our Commitment

We are committed to enhancing transparency and accountability in the UK's personal insolvency system and the wider landscape of financial vulnerability. Grounded in the public interest, we use only open data, process no personal information, and design all tools to support—not replace—regulatory oversight. By publishing clear indicators and accessible briefings, we aim to make the outcomes of debt policy visible, verifiable, and open to scrutiny.

Our initial focus was the IVA Protocol 2025, but our broader mission is to track systemic distress across low-income households, communities in persistent arrears, and groups underserved by existing support mechanisms.

Why We Exist

The outcomes of regulated debt solutions are often hidden from public view. Each year, tens of thousands enter IVAs—yet we know little about who completes them, who fails, or whether the system treats vulnerable clients fairly.

Aitia exists to change that. Through transparent indicators and simple visual tools, we make regulatory outcomes visible, measurable, and open to challenge.

Our Approach to Modelling and Risk

This project began as an experiment in predictive modelling—using neural networks and the logic of expected goals (xG) to detect early signs of insolvency. The aim was to measure pressure, not just outcomes, and to anticipate IVA failures before they occurred.

While this remains a long-term interest, our current focus is not prediction but monitoring. We now publish clear, real-time indicators—such as council tax arrears, surges in benefit claims, IVA failure rates, and access to advice. These are not forecasts but warning signals—designed to help charities, councils, regulators, and the public identify pressure points in the financial system.
